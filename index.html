MY SEARCH FOR OPEN SOURCE RESOURCES

by Ed Irvine

Background

I am relatively new to downloading files and apps off the internet. Because of my lack of knowledge regarding downloading from the web and my security concerns, I am hesitant to do this. However, I recognize that open source apps and downloads will be valuable tools and resources in my career. So, here we go!!

The Process

I will be going about this process in the following order:

  1. Contact a friend who is much more knowledgeable than me when it comes to computers
  2. Do a Google search regarding downloading do's and don'ts
  3. Start downloading and learn as I go

Contact from Friend

My friend suggested what i already assumed; download from a reputable source. However, he did inform me that I should extract any zipped files and then scan with my anti-virus after extraction, as some compressed folders cannot be scanned by antivirus software. I was unaware of this, so will be going through this process. His other suggestion was to create a virtual machine, which I am definitely NOT doing at this point.

Google Search

I found a very resourceful article on the wikiHow site that very much helped. First, it also mentioned to know the site. If it is reputable, and seems to be a quality site, then chances are it is safe to download from. It also said to watch when downloading for executable files, such as .exe, .bat, .pif, and .scr. Another warning was to check the file size, and if it was too small or too large then it may be harmful. The article was quite well written, and I trust it.

Actual Downloading

Since I require resources for the sample web-page I am developing for CPNT 260, I decided to download images and a logo that relate to my page's topic. Since I am going to be designing a page for a mock electrical contractor, the files I will be downloading will relate to this topic.

My first step was to review the Open Source Asset Cheat Sheet from the WBDV 2021 website. It lead me to Pixabay, and from there I found Pexel and Flickr. I was also able to find an SVG logo from a site called Free SVG.

It was a good experience. I learned some things, such as the fact that it is easy to click on images that are actually part of paid advertisements. But once I learned to navigate the sites, I realized how easy it is to download images that are licensed for any use. I also was pleased to find that the licenses were easy to find and interpret, which helped me confidently download the files.

Summary

My experience with this assignment has helped me begin to overcome a fear I have of using the internet and my computer. I have increased my knowledge of what downloading files and apps involves, and I will continue to do so.
